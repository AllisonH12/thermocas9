# p_trust sensitivity sweep — AUC under varying EvidenceClass base weights × ramp_n

Generated by `scripts/p_trust_sensitivity_sweep.py`. Addresses M2
from the third-party review (memo-2026-04-22-as): the manuscript
reports `p_trust` as one of the three V2.5 composite factors but
did not previously report sensitivity of validated-label AUC to
the per-`EvidenceClass` base-weight constants
(`_TRUST_BASE` in `src/thermocas/probabilistic.py`) or to the
saturating `min(1, n / ramp_n)` ramp threshold (default 30).

The sweep re-derives `p_therapeutic_selectivity` for every
record in each cohort's V2.5-diff scored JSONL using the
already-stored `p_targetable_tumor` and
`p_differential_protection` factors (the gap factor itself is
untouched; only `p_trust` is re-evaluated under each (weight
set × ramp_n) cell). AUC is mid-rank Mann–Whitney on the three
Roth Fig. 5d validated positives, computed against the cohort's
full chr5/6/10 negative universe.

## Interpretation

- **AUC is exactly invariant to `ramp_n` within each cohort.** The
  `p_trust` ramp is `min(1, min(n_t, n_n) / ramp_n)`; because each
  cohort's per-record `n_t` and `n_n` are constant (verified
  empirically: a single distinct `(n_t, n_n)` pair across 100k
  sampled records on every cohort), `ramp_n` reduces to a global
  multiplicative scale factor on every record's `p_therapeutic_selectivity`.
  Multiplicative scaling is rank-preserving, so AUC at the validated
  positives is identical across the entire `ramp_n ∈ {10, 20, 30,
  50, 100}` grid in every cohort. This is a structural property of
  the within-cohort uniform-`n` regime, not a numerical accident.
- **AUC is also nearly invariant to the EvidenceClass base-weight
  set on matched cell-line cohorts** (deltas ≤ 0.001 across shipped
  vs aggressive vs conservative). The reason is the top-100 window
  composition: it is 100/100 `exact` records on every matched
  cell-line cohort (`examples/evidence_class_distribution.md`), and
  all three weight sets agree at `EXACT = 0.95–0.99`, so the
  ranking inside the top of the score distribution is dominated by
  the `p_targ × p_diff` factor rather than by `p_trust`.
- **GSE69914 tissue is the exception.** Its top-100 splits 33
  EXACT / 67 PROXIMAL_CLOSE, and the three weight sets disagree at
  `PROXIMAL_CLOSE` (0.50 / 0.75 / 0.85). The conservative set edges
  ahead of shipped by +0.003 (0.7760 vs 0.7727) by relatively
  down-weighting PROXIMAL_CLOSE; the aggressive set under-performs
  shipped by −0.006 (0.7668 vs 0.7727). All three sets stay within
  ±0.01 of shipped.
- Practical consequence: the shipped (0.95/0.75/0.45/0.15) ×
  `ramp_n = 30` configuration is not globally AUC-optimal at the
  validated tier, but the gap to the per-cohort best is ≤ 0.006 on
  every cohort. We have not retuned the defaults; the shipped
  setting is documented as conservative-by-design and reproducible
  across runs. A continuous-trust replacement for the discrete
  `EvidenceClass` bins is on the §6.3 follow-up list (PAPER.md).
